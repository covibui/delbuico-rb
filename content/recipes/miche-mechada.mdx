title: Miche's Carne Mechada
author: Miche
group: meat
prep_time:
  time: 20
  unit: minute
cook_time:
  time: 30
  unit: minute
servings: 4
ingredients:
  - item: 2lbs. Flank steak /skirt / short plate-
  - item: "Enough beef broth to cover the beef "
  - item: Salt and pepper to taste
  - item: "3 cloves garlic "
  - item: Sprig of parsley
  - item: "Stick of celery "
  - item: ½ small to med onion
  - item: "½ red bell pepper "
  - item: "1 stick green onion  "
  - item: "Liquify in a blender: (4 tomatoes seedless and chopped, 1 red pepper
      seedless and chopped, 1 yellow onion chopped)   "
    substitute: "Or (1 16 oz can crushed tomatoes, 1 6 oz can tomato paste) "
  - item: "1/4 annatto oil  "
  - item: "1 teaspoon salt "
  - item: "4 garlic cloves "
  - item: "1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper "
  - item: "2 teaspoons pikliz "
  - item: 1/2 c dry white wine
  - item: "1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce "
  - item: "2 teaspoons cumin  "
  - item: "1/2 tablespoon dried oregano "
  - item: 2 bay leaves
directions:
  - step: >-
      
      First, season the flank steak with salt and pepper. Then, in a pressure cooker, place the flank steak with ingredients and the broth beef stock.  Pressure cook for 15 minutes.
  - step: >-
      
      Remove the flank steak from the broth. DO NOT discard the beef broth.  Now, let the beef rest for a few minutes. Then shred the beef using two forks to separate the strands. If the beef is not easily shredded, you can cook it for an additional 5 minutes.
  - step: >-
      
      In a large skillet over medium heat the oil. Sauté onion and pepper for 3-5 minutes, or until onion is translucent. Add garlic and let it cook for 1 more minute. Remove onions and peppers set aside.
  - step: >-
      
      Place the tomatoes, onion and red pepper in a blender to liquefy and then add to the preheated oil. (or just add tomato sauce and paste) Cook for about 3 minutes in the garlic on medium heat. Stir in shredded beef and the rest of the ingredients mixing well to incorporate all ingredients. Add the bay leaves. Cover and let simmer at medium-low heat, stirring often, for around 30 minutes until the sauce has thickened and all the flavors are incorporated into the beef. Add salt or pepper to taste if necessary. Garnish with Onion and pepper.
